Wildlife Rehab

BNC's Rehabilitation Policy

The purpose of BNC's wildlife rehabilitation program is to educate people regarding the natural history of Ohio's wildlife, to offer help and advice when wildlife and people conflict, and to care for, rehabilitate and release native Ohio wildlife expected to survive in the natural environment. The BNC Wildlife Rehabilitation Unit cannot accept non-native invasive species such as European starlings, house sparrows and pigeons or any domestic or exotic species. Our Rehab Unit accepts injured or orphaned native wildlife daily from 1:00 - 4:00pm.

4 Steps to Animal Rescue

Is the animal...

  • clearly injured?
    Call to speak with one of our staff in our wildlife rehab unit at (937) 698-6493.
  • sick or acting strangely?
    Call your County Wildlife Officer or police non-emergency number.
  • an uninjured nestling?
    Simply return the young animal to its nest.
  • an older uninjured youngster?
    Please leave it alone and keep pets and children away so it isn't scared!

Many wild animals are taken from their homes in error. If you have any questions concerning wildlife in need, please call Brukner Nature Center at 937-698-6493 first! We are available 7 days a week to answer your calls. If you are calling after 5pm, you will be directed to call our emergency after hours advice line.
